[h1]STS2 Mod Translator[/h1]
Translate the text of [b]other mods[/b] into [b]any language[/b] — right inside the game, without rebuilding them.

[i]A utility/tool mod. It reads the localized text of your other installed mods and lets you supply your own translations, which are injected back into the game at runtime — cards, relics, powers, characters and more, even for mods that don't ship your language.[/i]

[hr][/hr]

[h2]How to use[/h2]
[olist]
[*]Enable this mod and launch the game in the language you want to translate into.
[*]Open [b]Main Menu ▸ Mod Translator[/b] (it sits next to Settings).
[*]Drill down: pick a [b]mod[/b] ▸ a [b]language[/b] ▸ a [b]file[/b].
[*]Translate in the built-in editor, or [b]Upload[/b] a JSON file, or [b]Open Folder[/b] to edit externally.
[*]Press [b]Save[/b] — it validates and applies instantly. No restart needed.
[/olist]

[h2]In-game editor[/h2]
[list]
[*][b]Side-by-side panes[/b] — original text (read-only) on the left, your translation on the right
[*][b]Reference language toggle[/b] — if a mod already ships other languages, switch the left pane between them (e.g. compare against the existing Chinese/English)
[*][b]Line numbers[/b] for easy navigation, with JSON validation on save
[*][b]Upload[/b] a translation file, or [b]Open Folder[/b] / [b]Reload[/b] for an external workflow
[/list]

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*]Drill-down panel: Mods ▸ Languages ▸ Files
[*]Per-mod / per-language progress (coverage %)
[*]Auto-generated templates — original text for reference + empty fields to fill
[*]Untranslated entries safely fall back to the original text
[/list]

[h2]Share translations as a standalone mod[/h2]
Turn your work into its own mod — just like other mods reference [b]baselib[/b], a [i]translation mod[/i] references this one. Other players install your translation mod (plus this one) and the text applies automatically, with no editing.
[list]
[*]Pick a mod, then press [b]Export as mod[/b] — it writes a ready-to-ship mod folder (manifest + your translations for every language) to [i]%APPDATA%\Sts2ModTranslator\exported[/i].
[*]Drop that folder into [i]<STS2>/mods/[/i] or upload it to the Workshop to share.
[*]No DLL or coding needed — a translation mod is pure data plus a manifest that declares [b]dependencies: ["Sts2ModTranslator"][/b].
[*]Installed translation packs are detected at boot and applied last, so your own in-editor edits still win over a pack, and a pack wins over the original text.
[/list]

[h2]Where the files are[/h2]
Translations live under the mod's [i]Translations[/i] folder (use [b]Open Folder[/b]). Each mod/language has editable [i]overrides[/i] plus a read-only [i]source[/i] reference of the original text.

[h2]Translation tips[/h2]
Keep these intact and change only the human-readable text:
[list]
[*]Placeholders like [b]!D![/b], [b]!B![/b], [b]!Crit![/b] — numbers are substituted in automatically
[*]Markup such as [b]*[/b]keyword, leading [b]#[/b], [b]/+[/b], and [b][color][/b] tags
[/list]
Leave a value [b]empty[/b] to keep the original (English) text for that entry.

[h2]Scope & compatibility[/h2]
[list]
[*]Works with mods that ship a standard localization folder — most well-made content mods (e.g. custom characters).
[*]Mods that hardcode text outside the localization system are listed as [i]unsupported[/i].
[*][b]No load-order requirement[/b] — translations are always applied last automatically.
[/list]
